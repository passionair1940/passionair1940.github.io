This part of the site is dedicated to the French Naval Aviation in 1939-40. It complements the main site "French Air Force", which is accessible at any time via the cockade at the top right of each page. This Weapon, whose action is less well known, nevertheless deserves a tribute. Its men fought with merit, often employed in missions for which they were not prepared. ,
This part of the site is composed of 330 pages in French, and is entirely available in English. Some sections remain to be built, and will be completed over time and research.


Here is the detail of the contents:

- Naval Aviation:
- Fleets :
The units of the Naval Aviation are divided administratively by Fleets: you will find here the detail of this division
- Aircrafts :
44 of the main French aircrafts, land or seaplanes, are described here : characteristics, history, photos, list, aircraft, ...
- Squadrons :
60 Squadrons are listed here, all specialities included.
- Pilots :
To date, 293 pilots and crew members are on my lists. They are listed alphabetically and by weapon. This list, still very thin today, will be completed regularly and will never be, as for the Air Force, exhaustive...

Citations for Naval Aviation members will be available soon.

- FORCES INVOLVED :
You will find here an overview of the Aircraft used by the different intelligentsia whether they are Axis (Germany and Italy) or Allied (Great Britain, Belgium, Holland): 40 foreign Aircraft are presented in these pages.
- CAMPAIGN OF FRANCE :
Location of Units :
This section is not yet documented.
Victory Report :
You will find here, the balance sheet of the victories of the French Naval Aviation. This balance sheet can appear weak compared to that of the Air Force, but it was obtained on outdated equipment and under difficult conditions of engagement, and in numerical inferiority.
Casualty Report:
I drew up in these pages, a balance sheet of the losses of the French units of the Naval Aeronautics in order to illustrate if need be the heavy toll paid by the French crews.
- MANUFACTURERS:
Here will be briefly presented, the French manufacturers of Airplanes, who will never really meet the needs of the French Aviation in full mutation. These will in fact be destabilized by the multitude of rearmament plans and recent nationalizations. They will, however, provide some fine achievements, but the events will unfortunately prevent them from demonstrating their qualities.
