Torpedo Squadrons T-4

Historical

The T-4 Squadron is created on January 15, 1940 under the command of Lieutenant Camard. Based on the pond of Berre, it is equipped with Latécoère 298.

Like many units, the first months of the war are devoted to training. The aircraft of the unit are nevertheless equipped with two-blade wooden propellers with fixed pitch, usually used for transfers .... It is only from April that the Latécoère 298 of the Squadron will be equipped with Ratier propeller with variable pitch. In May 1940, declared operational, the squadron T4 is attached to the F5T flotilla. After the German attack of May 10th, 1940, the T-4 carries out missions of surveillance over the mediterranean, until the entry to the war of Italy.

An attack will be conducted on the night of June 17 to 18, 1940 against Italian targets. The crews will join their base without losses. This will be the only mission against the Italian forces before the Armistice.

The T-4 squadron will be renamed 4T from August 1st, 1940.
