Fighter Group GARC I/561 & GC II/10
3rd Squadron
4th Squadron (Ex SPA76)

Historical

The GARC 561 (Regional Air fighting Group) is formed on January 1, 1938 and is the continuation of the Ex ERC I / 561, unit designed to train reserve pilots.

At the outbreak of hostilities, the Group, which is based at Rouen, is made up of one-third active pilots and the rest of the unit is made up of pilots of reserves mobilized. The unit entered the war equipped with Nieuport 62/622 and Dewoitine D501 for the 2nd Sqn. He will not receive his first Bloch 151 and 152 until October 23, 1939.

On January 11, 1940, GARC I / 561 became GC II / 10.

On the date of the outbreak of the German attack of May 10, 1940, the GC II / 10 belongs to the No.21 Fighting Group of the ZOAN (North Air Operations Area)

He actively participates in the Campaign of France and his palmares will be 16 victories and 3 probable probable.

GC II / 10 is dissolved on August 16, 1940
