Fighter Group GC III/3
5th Squadron (Ex SPA 150)
6th Squadron

Historical

Following the decision to add a third Group to existing Fighting Squadrons, GC III / 3 was established in May 1939 with elements of GC I / 3 and GC II / 3, and thus does not incorporate any existing tradition.

This group was marked by a serious accident during a transfer between Dijon and Salon en Provence mobilization on August 28: 3 pilots crash on the ground and 3 others are seriously injured. He fights on MORANE MS406 before being transformed on DEWOITINE D520 at the end of May 1940.

On the date of the outbreak of the German attack of May 10, 1940, the GC III / 3 belongs to the Fighting Group No. 21 of the ZOAN (North Air Operations Area).

On June 19, 1940, the GC III / 3 crosses the Mediterranean with 22 Dewoitine D520. The Group is finally dissolved on August 25, 1940.

The GC III / 3 will finish the campaign in France with a record of 33 Victories sure, including 25 on MS406, and 9 probabilities including 8 on MS406. This will be done at the cost of 12 Kills and 12 Wounded.

Aircrafts

The GC III/3 was equipped since its creation on May 1939, following aircrafts :

- Dewoitine D500 / D501
- Potez 630 / 631
- Morane-Saulnier MS406
- Dewoitine D520 from end of May 1940
