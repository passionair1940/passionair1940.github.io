Fighter Group GC III/5
5th Squadron (Ex BR 108)
6th Squadron (Ex SPA 97)

Historical

GC III / 5 was created on May 15, 1940 by uniting squadrons BR 108 and SPA 97. At the declaration of hostilities, GC III / 5 occupies the airfield of Bizerte-Sidi Ahmed (Tunisia) and is equipped with Morane Saulnier 406 and Bleriot-Spad 510.

With the Groups engaged at the front having priority, GC III / 5 suffers from lack of equipment and means. The fighting against the Italians from June 12, 1940, will often be without results: the Moranes are too slow and the means of communication failing or missing.

At the armistice, the GC III / 5 was a likely victory against an Italian plane.

GC III / 5 is finally dissolved on August 1st, 1940.
