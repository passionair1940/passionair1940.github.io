4th Squadron

Historical

GC II / 9 was born in North Africa on November 1, 1939, following the dissolution of AFN's Marching Wing. In the mobilization, many of its pilots will swell the ranks of the Fighting units in France. Only experienced pilots remain on site.

At the end of January 1940, young pilots from Fighting schools who had just been dropped on MS406, were able to reconstitute GC II / 9 on the basis of Oran. On March 17, Commander ROLLET, boss of the Group, receives the order to prepare the movement of his Group towards the metropolis. The first elements embark to join Marseille-Marginane on April 13, followed by the other pilots on April 17. The GC II / 9 arrives in mainland France without planes, and is equipped in May 1940 with BLOCH MB152. During this transformation period, it is attached to the ZOAA (Alpine Air Operations Zone)

After the Armistice, the GC II / 9 is part of the Groups maintained in the Vichy Air Force on BLOCH MB152. GC II / 9 is dissolved on November 28, 1942

Aircrafts

The GC II/9 was equipped since its creation the November 1st, 1939, following aircrafts :

- Morane-Saulnier MS406 from november 1939 to May 1940
- Bloch MB152 & 155 from May 1940 until the dissolution of the Group in 1942
The used aircrafts are described in the accessible file by the link below. You will find Nb of series, Registration, date of affectation to the group, and fate of the plane there.

In this list are identified :

    - 29 Morane-Saulnier MS406
    - 50 Bloch MB152
    - 1 Bloch MB155
