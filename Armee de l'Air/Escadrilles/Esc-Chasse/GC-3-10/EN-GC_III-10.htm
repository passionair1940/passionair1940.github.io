5th Squadron (Ex ERC 3/561)
6th Squadron(Ex ERC 4/561)

Historical

ERC 3/561 was born on August 24, 1939, from the dissolution of the Villacoublay Air Rescue Training Group, or Group 591. This Group consisted of a Fighter Squadron, a Bomb Squadron and a Training Squadron. At the dissolution of this group, the Fighter Squadron is divided into two, one of which is called ERC 3/561. Composed of experienced pilots, this unit is based upon mobilization on the Calais base. The two squadrons met October 14, 1939 to become the GARC II / 561.

The GARC starts the war with old-fashioned equipment composed of Nieuport 622 and Blériot-Spad 510. He will not receive his first Bloch 151 until December 1939. The last Blériot-Spad 510 will follow the Group throughout the campaign (N ° 24 and 32)

The GARC II / 561 becomes the GC III / 10 on January 18, 1940. It participates actively in the campaign of France and its palmares, in Armisitce will rise to 13 Victories sure and 3 probable.

On the date of the outbreak of the German attack of May 10, 1940, the GC III / 10 belongs to the Hunting Group No. 21 of the ZOAN (North Air Operations Area)

The Group is finally dissolved on July 24, 1940.
