5th Squadron (Ex SPA 96)
6th Squadron (Ex SPA 12)

Historical

Before becoming GC III / 9, the group was created in 1936 as a "Regional Air Group" to allow Reservists to fly under the leadership of active cadres. On February 1st, 1937, Commander VIGUIER took the leadership of the Group which became the GARC I / 562 (Regional Air Fighting Group), with an Escadrille led by Captain BILLON.

At the outbreak of hostilities, the Group is mobilized on Dewoitine D501. On September 25, 1939, born a second Squadron, the 2/562 commanded by Captain CHEVALIER.

The transformation on Bloch MB152 begins in November 1939. On January 11, 1940, the Group officially becomes GC III / 9.

On the date of the outbreak of the German attack of May 10, 1940, the GC III / 9 is attached to the Air Forces Reserved ZOAA (Zone d'Aériennes Alpes)

The Group was first dissolved on July 6, 1940, then recreated in August 1940 with GC III/1 and GC I/6 pilots dissolved on August 10, 1940, and some pilots of GC II/6 and GC II/2 also dissolved. The 2nd Squadron emblem shown above is in fact that of the 2nd Squadron of GC I/6 of Commander Tricaud. The latter, former GC I/6 boss and new GC III / 9 Commanding Officer, will adopt the 1st and 2nd Squadron emblems of his former GC I/6 Group to GC III/9.

The Group is finally dissolved on December 4, 1942
