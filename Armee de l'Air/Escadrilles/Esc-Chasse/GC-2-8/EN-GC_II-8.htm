Fighter Group GC II/8
3rd Squadron (Ex 3C1)
4th Squadron (Ex SPA 38)

Historical

The GC II / 8 was born from the integration of the 3C1 squadron of the Navy in the Air Force on January 1st, 1936: it becomes the 3rd Squadron of GC II / 8 which will be joined on January 1st, 1939, by the 4iem Squadron which takes up the traditions of SPA 38.

At the declaraton of the War, the GC II / 8 is equipped with Potez 631. In November 1939, the group is placed at the disposal of the Admiralty on the ground of Calais, in order to prevent the action of Seaplanes at sea North. They are leasing mines and sinking several merchant ships.

It is only at the end of January that the 3rd Eszcadrille GC II / 8 begins to be transformed on BLOCH MB152, the 4th Squadron will not perceive its single engine in April.

The Group, stationed on the grounds of the North of France, ensures in particular the cover of the Dunkirk pocket, before falling back in front of the German advance

GC II / 8 is one of six groups maintained in service after the Armistice. The Group is finally dissolved on November 25, 1942
