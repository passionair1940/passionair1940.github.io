Fighter Group GC I/145 "Varsovie"

Historical

On January 22, 1940, General Zajac, Polish General in Exile, obtained the agreement, by the French government, for the creation of a Polish Fighter Group to help the Finns in their war against the USSR. But the Armistice between the two countries intervenes before the end of the Training of the Group. On April 15, this becomes GC I/145. The training of the pilots is carried out on MS406, but the final endowment will be based on Caudron Cr714. During its training period, GC I/145 is attached to the ZOAA (Alpine Air Operation Zone).

On May 19th, the Group moves to Villacoublay and takes into account its Caudron who prove to be very under-powered Fighters. On June 2, the Group moved to Dreux and is in charge of protecting Paris. The missions on alerts are often useless: the Cr714 does not climb fast enough to join the attackers.

On June 11, 1940, GC I/145 moved near Etampes, then to Chateauroux on June 14th. After joining Rochefort where they provide a time defense of the ground, the pilots finally board to Britain on June 19th.
