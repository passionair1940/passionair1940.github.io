Historical

On March 20, 1940, GB II / 35 was assigned to Assault Aviation and became GBA II / 35. On May 10, 1940, the GBA II / 35 belongs to the Groupement n° 19 of the Southeast Bombardment Aviation Training Group of the ZOAA (Alpine Air Operations Zone).

However, in the face of GBA losses in combat, the GBA II / 35 is removed from active units to provide support to the other four Assault Aviation Groups. The GBA I / 54 receives 15 crews and 4 Breguet 691, the GBA II / 54 receives him 10 crews. The band is dissolved on August 30, 1940.

Crew PASQUIER Armand
Sergeant
Pilot
 Amiot 143 n° 110 : During a reconnaissance flight on the night of December 2 to 3, 1939, the aircraft hit the ground in the forest of "La Joux" (Jura) because of fog. All the crew was killed except Sergeant CHARBONNIER, who was ejected at 35 meters of altitude when the aircraft hit the trees. Here is an excerpt from Sergeant Charbonnier's account, with, in brackets, the comments of Mrs. Roussel, his daughter:
"We were lost in the fog, we followed the railroad tracks to find our bearings, then...tunnel...then the descent of the plane after losing an engine and falling on the President fir tree,(the highest in the Forêt de la Joux), the moment I turned on my headlamp and finally the shock on the ground. Barking dogs in the distance, who came closer"...
(no doubt accompanied by farmers: Then the great white hole, and weeks, or months (?) later, waking up surrounded by the horns of the nuns of the Hospital of Pontarlier who were circling around him, and finally convinced him to learn the hymns because his deep voice made the stained glass windows of the chapel vibrate on Sundays!...".)
