Prototype VG33
01
 Between its tests at Villacoublay and its taking into account by the Air Force on 1 June 1940, Prototype No. 01 will receive a new segmented camouflage engine hood, an OLAER train with its traps and a pitot tube as standard. It is also the only one to have a khaki propeller cone. Recovered by the Defense Patrol of Etampes, it will be folded over Bordeaux and then served in GC I / 55. It is on this terrain that he will be captured by the Germans. This aircraft is recognizable from its propeller pan painted in Khaki. It is also coded "4" White on the rudder.
Arsenal VG33
1
 Taken into account on June 6th. Folded around June 15 by the Defense Patrol of Etampes on the grounds of Clermont-Ferrand. He remained there until the Armistice, assigned to the EAA 301 in waiting for repairs.
Arsenal VG33
2
 Taken into account on June 9th. According to the historian Jean Cuny, this aircraft would have been folded on Blagnac by the pilots of the patrol of Etampes
Arsenal VG33
3
 Taken into account on June 6th. This aircraft received the fuselage of the VG-31 which was never assembled. No info on this aircraft. It does not appear on any Airfield at the armistice and does not appear on any Flight log consulted. It has either been left at Villacoublay or Orléans or is one of the two copies present at Bordeaux at the Armistice
Arsenal VG33
4
 Taken into account on June 9th. As No. 2, this aircraft was evacuated on Blagnac by pilots of the patrol of Etampes
Arsenal VG33
5
 No. 5 was escorted from Etampes to Clermont Ferrand on June 15, but he can not be found at the Armistice ... The Germans will actually capture him when they enter Clermont on June 21. It is probable that No. 5 is the only VG33 caught by the Germans in flight condition, so it will be evaluated at the Rechlin Test Center with code 3 + 5
Arsenal VG33
6
 Taken into account on June 6th. No. 6 was probably evacuated with Nos. 1 and 5, since it is in Clermont at the Armistice. This aircraft is in fact intended to serve as reproduction model for the Michelin workshops which were to reproduce the plane under License. He will always be in Clermont end 40 at the EAA 301, deboned by Michelin's mechanics. It bears a white "6" number on the rudder.
Arsenal VG33
7
 It is surely the most photographed Arsenal and therefore the less mysterious. From Etampes, he was transferred on 13 June to Orléans and then to Bordeaux in the GC I / 55 in the hands of Lt LESPINASSE. He will be repatriated to Francazal on June 25 where he is put in storage. It is one of the few VG33 to have been complete
Arsenal VG33

 Not much information on the n ° 8. He could be one of the two present in Bordeaux at the Armistice. In this case, n ° 3 must remain in Villacoublay
Arsenal VG33

 Folded by the Etampes Patrol on the airfield of Orléans on June 13, 1940, then towards Bordeaux where it is found at the Armistice
