Captain Georges GARDE

Biography

Born July 30, 1903 in Tarare, in the Rhone, Georges Garde voluntarily engages in the Air Force on August 17, 1921. Four months later, he entered at Istres where he obtained his License on April 22, 1922. He joined the 35th Aviation Regiment in Lyon. Promoted Sergeant in January 1924, he obtained his transfer to French West Africa in 1926 and took part in the exploration missions to Senegal and Sudan and to the Mauritania Campaign on Breguet 19.

Returning to France in 1928, the Adjutant Garde was assigned in May 1929 to the 37th Aviation Regiment stationed in Morocco. With this unit, he carries out several actions against dissidents of the High Atlas on Potez TOE. Appointed Sub-Lieutenant on April 23, 1931, he was posted to Meknes where he obtained his observer's certificate by plane. Returning to France in 1932, he was transfered to the 1st Wing Fighter of Villacoublay before taking part in the "Black Cruise" from November 1933 to January 1934. Returned to 1st Wing on his return, he was promoted to Captain in 1938 and receives his certificate as patrol leader.

On September 3, 1939, the Captain commanded 2nd Squadron of GC I / 1. Detached in August 1939 at the CEMA (Test Center of Aerial Equipment) of Reims, he was dropped on Bloch MB 152 in August 1939, aircraft that eventually equip his unit to replace the Dewoitine 510 it has at the declaration of war.

Some feats of arm

On 5 September, accompanied by Sub Lieutenant Lieutenant SCHMIDT and Sergeant DUBOST, Captain GARDE, then Commanding Officer of 2nd Squadron GC I / 1 makes the first war mission of the group on venerable Dewoitine D510, fortunately without making bad meetings ...

On May 14, 1940, Captain GARDE attacks a group of 18 BF110 with his patrol. He managed to burn the engine of a BF 110, but himself attacked, he must clear. This BF 110 will be credited to him. In the same day, while he releases a BLOCH 152 attacked by BF 110, his weapons are jammed, and he is himself touched to the engine: he manages to put his BLOCH n ° 425 near Maubeuge.Il will join the Group the next day after many events: it is even stopped by Belgian railwaymen who take it for a German paratrooper, to be finally released by the Gendarmerie.

On May 17, 1940, Captain GARDE conducted a cover mission with a GC I / 1 patrol. The patrol intercepts a HENSCHEL HE126 observation that ignites and explodes. This victory is attributed to the three pilots of the patrol.

On May 20, 1940, Captain GARDE commanded a double patrol of the GC I / 1. He attacks a DORNIER DO215 who is hidden in the clouds. At each reappearance, the German bomber is awaited by Captain GARDE who, his collimator broken down, pulls him with his hood sight. DO215 crashes near Verberie

On June 15, 1940, the GC I / 1 sends 4 simple patrols that succeed one another throughout the day for a coverage of the Chateauroux area. Captain GARDE takes a triple patrol. It attacked a HENSCHEL HE126 and shot it near Orleans. The crew managed to evacuate the aircraft and was captured on the ground.

On June 20, 1940, Captain GARDE took off with three other pilots in pursuit of HEINKEL HE111 training en route to Bordeaux. One of the bombers is hit and his engine right in flames, it lands in Saujon, on the other side of the Gironde. His crew is taken prisoner.
