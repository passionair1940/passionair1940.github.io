Adjutant Emile BECQUET

Biography

Emile Becquet was born on June 8, 1914 in Le Derzeele, in the North. He is a mechanic in the civil when he decides to commit the 15 of October of 1935 in the Air force to the 3rd Battalion of the Air. Appointed Sergeant, he was posted on 1 September 1936 to the 4th Squadron of GC II / 1, which is based in Etampes. In the autumn of 1937, he and his group conducted a 3-month maneuvering campaign in North Africa, after which he was sent back to civilian life. He joined his squadron with the rank of Adjutant during the mobilization of August 1939 and resumed his place at the GC II / 1 which moved to Buc. The unit is still equipped with Dewoitine 510, totally outdated. Its main mission is to cover the Paris region.

On September 13, 1939, Warrant Officer BECQUET left temporarily in training at Orléans-Bricy to join an experimental squadron of Bloch 152. Adjutant BECQUET will accidentally kill himself on July 12, 1940 during the convoy of a Bloch MB152

Some feats of arm

On May 20, 1940, Adjutant BECQUET took off for a cover mission on a light patrol from GC II / 1. A GC II / 9 patrol accompanies them. Alerted by a shot of DCA, they spot a DORNIER DO215 carrying out a reconnaissance mission. It turns and stings to the ground to escape. The French pilots are spoiled by the swirl of the German aircraft, which sticks to the relief while defending dearly its rear sector with its back-gunner. It is finally GC II / 9's Staff-Sergeant CUCUMEL who gives the coup de grace. The victory is credited to the four French pilots

On June 11, 1940, Adjutant BECQUET participated in a destruction mission with a GC II / 1 patrol. In collaboration with Adjutant RICHARDIN, they join a HENSCHEL HE126 and shoot him down.
