Staff-Sergeant Louis COADER

Biography

Louis COADER was born on August 29, 1913 in the 17th Arrondissement in Paris
He was killed in aerial combat on May 20, 1940

Some feats of arm

On 21 December 1939, during a transfer flight to Marignane for a shooting campaign, a patrol of three GC I/1 aircraft commanded by Staff-Sergeant COALDER was surprised by the fog. The very bad conditions force the patrol to land on a makeshift terrain near Autun. The three BLOCH MB152 are destroyed. Sergeant LEPROVOST and Staff-Sergeant COADER are unharmed, but he is injured.

On May 12, 1940, Staff-Sergeant COADER took a section of GC I / 1 to attack three DORNIER DO215s. They attack the enemy formation from behind, but the bombers defend themselves fiercely and drop their bombs to flee. If the left winger, touched, leaves his section, the French can not pursue him for lack of ammunition. Victory will be counted as probable to the patrol.

On May 15, 1940, Sergeant Chief COADER performs a mission within a GC I / 1 patrol covering a sector. The French pilots see a DORNIER DO17 trying to escape to the ground, but he is joined and his right engine catches fire under the attacks of the four hunters. The German bomber will crash on the ground. The victory is credited to the four members of the patrol.

On May 17, 1940, Staff-Sergeant COADER takes part in a swirling fight that opposes a GC I / 1 aircraft with BF 109E. Although in a delicate situation, he managed to break free and shoot down a BF 109E.

On May 20, 1940, Staff-Sergeant COADER, on a mission with a GC I / 1 patrol, met a large formation of German bombers escorted by BF 110. Despite the importance of training, he and his teammate enemy aircraft. His teammate, Sergeant LEPROVOST, manages to free himself by a spin of the escort BF110, but the Staff-Sergeant COADER is shot down ... While he was trying to evacuate in parachute at low altitude, the command cable of his canvas breaks, and he has no time to operate the emergency handle: his body will be found near his BLOCH MB 152 n ° 391
