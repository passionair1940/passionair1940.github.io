Lieutenant SCHMIDT

Biography

 

Some feats of arm

On the 5th of September, accompanied by Sergeant DUBOST and Captain GARDE, Sub-Lieutenant SCHMIDT, then pilot at the 2nd Squadron of GC I/1, made the first war mission of the group on venerable Dewoitine D510, fortunately without making bad encounters. ..

On February 13th, 1940, the GC I/1 personnel carrier had an accident and the wounded Lieutenant SCHMIDT would not return to the Group until May 11, 1940.

On May 14, 1940, Lieutenant SCHMIDT, with a GC I/1 patrol attacks a aircraft of 18 BF 110. He pursues one of them up to 500m from the ground and manages to touch it. But, he himself was hit and continued by 5 BF 110 to the ground. He managed, however, to put his BLOCH MB152 Nb 569 near Couvron. The aircraft is burned by its pilot. He receives his group by car the same day. He goes back 3 hours later in a plane, and by releasing a POTEZ 63-11, he manages to shoot down a BF 110.

On May 15, 1940, during a Bombardiers LeO 451 escort mission, he was hit and had to put his BLOCH MB 152 Nb 175 on his belly.

On May 21, 1940, during a severe fight against a formation of BF110, the Lieutenant SCHMIDT is wounded in the legs and is evacuated on the Hospital of Senlis. He will join his group only a few days before the Armistice on June 23, 1940.
